# Founder Playbooks

## Purpose

Founder Playbooks provides a structured operating system for building, launching, and scaling startups in the Solana ecosystem.

Rather than offering isolated advice, this framework guides founders through each stage of the startup lifecycle with clear objectives, success criteria, common mistakes, and next actions.

---

# Stage 1 — Discover

## Objective

Determine whether the problem is worth solving.

## Key Questions

* What problem are we solving?
* Who experiences this problem?
* How painful is the problem?
* How are people solving it today?
* Why is Solana relevant?

## Deliverables

* Problem statement
* Ideal customer profile (ICP)
* Initial market map
* Validation hypotheses

## Success Criteria

* Clear customer pain point
* Defined target audience
* Evidence of demand
* Strong market opportunity

## Common Mistakes

* Starting with technology instead of a problem
* Building before validation
* Targeting everyone
* Ignoring competitors

## Next Stage

Move to Market Research.

---

# Stage 2 — Market Research

## Objective

Understand the competitive landscape.

## Key Questions

* Who are the competitors?
* What are they doing well?
* What gaps exist?
* What unique advantage can we build?

## Deliverables

* Competitor analysis
* Market opportunity map
* Positioning statement

## Success Criteria

* Clear differentiation
* Market opportunity identified

## Common Mistakes

* Underestimating competition
* Copying existing products
* Ignoring user feedback

## Next Stage

Move to Customer Discovery.

---

# Stage 3 — Customer Discovery

## Objective

Validate assumptions with real users.

## Key Questions

* Who are the users?
* What are their goals?
* What are their frustrations?
* What outcomes matter most?

## Deliverables

* User interviews
* ICP refinement
* Pain-point prioritization

## Success Criteria

* Consistent user feedback patterns
* Validated assumptions

## Common Mistakes

* Leading interview questions
* Confirmation bias
* Small sample sizes

## Next Stage

Move to MVP Design.

---

# Stage 4 — Build (MVP)

## Objective

Create the smallest product capable of validating assumptions.

## Key Questions

* What is the minimum valuable product?
* Which features are essential?
* What can wait?

## Deliverables

* MVP scope
* Product roadmap
* Success metrics

## Success Criteria

* Fast development cycle
* Clear learning objectives

## Common Mistakes

* Overbuilding
* Feature creep
* Perfectionism

## Next Stage

Move to Fundraising or Launch.

---

# Stage 5 — Fundraise

## Objective

Acquire resources to accelerate growth.

## Key Questions

* Why are we raising?
* How much capital is needed?
* Which investors fit best?

## Deliverables

* Pitch deck
* Investor list
* Fundraising narrative

## Success Criteria

* Clear use of funds
* Strong investor pipeline

## Common Mistakes

* Raising too early
* Raising without traction
* Weak storytelling

## Next Stage

Move to Launch.

---

# Stage 6 — Launch

## Objective

Successfully bring the product to market.

## Key Questions

* Who should use this first?
* What channels will drive adoption?
* How will success be measured?

## Deliverables

* Launch plan
* Distribution strategy
* KPI dashboard

## Success Criteria

* Initial user adoption
* Positive retention signals

## Common Mistakes

* Launching without distribution
* Ignoring onboarding
* Measuring vanity metrics

## Next Stage

Move to Growth.

---

# Stage 7 — Grow

## Objective

Build repeatable acquisition and retention systems.

## Key Questions

* Which channels work?
* What drives retention?
* What growth loops exist?

## Deliverables

* Growth roadmap
* Community systems
* Partnership pipeline

## Success Criteria

* Consistent user growth
* Healthy retention
* Community engagement

## Common Mistakes

* Chasing every channel
* Over-relying on incentives
* Ignoring retention

## Next Stage

Move to Token Strategy (if needed).

---

# Stage 8 — Token

## Objective

Design sustainable token systems.

## Key Questions

* Does the product need a token?
* What utility exists?
* How are incentives aligned?

## Deliverables

* Token strategy
* Distribution model
* Incentive framework

## Success Criteria

* Clear utility
* Sustainable economics
* Aligned incentives

## Common Mistakes

* Launching a token too early
* Financial engineering without utility
* Unsustainable emissions

## Next Stage

Move to Scale.

---

# Stage 9 — Scale

## Objective

Build durable systems and organizations.

## Key Questions

* What breaks at 10x growth?
* What processes need automation?
* What KPIs matter most?

## Deliverables

* Operating systems
* KPI framework
* Team structure

## Success Criteria

* Operational efficiency
* Predictable execution
* Sustainable growth

## Common Mistakes

* Hiring too quickly
* Lack of processes
* KPI overload

---

# Founder Rule

At every stage:

Validate before building.

Retain before scaling.

Create value before extracting value.

Build systems before adding complexity.
