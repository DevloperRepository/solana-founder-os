# Product Strategy

## Purpose

Product strategy answers a simple question:

What should we build, for whom, and why?

Most startups fail because they build features instead of solving problems.

The purpose of product strategy is to focus resources on the highest-leverage opportunities.

---

# Product Strategy Framework

A strong product strategy consists of:

* Customer
* Problem
* Solution
* Distribution
* Advantage

---

# Customer

Define:

* Who is the primary user?
* Who is the buyer?
* Who benefits most?

Avoid building for everyone.

Specificity creates clarity.

---

# Problem

Questions:

* What pain point exists?
* How frequently does it occur?
* How expensive is it?
* What happens if it remains unsolved?

The bigger the pain, the easier the adoption.

---

# Solution

A product should:

* Solve one problem exceptionally well
* Deliver value quickly
* Reduce friction

Avoid feature accumulation.

---

# Strategic Positioning

Complete:

For [customer]

Who wants [outcome]

Our product provides [solution]

Unlike [alternative]

Because [advantage]

---

# Product Roadmap

Prioritize:

1. Core value creation
2. User activation
3. Retention
4. Expansion

Avoid prioritizing vanity features.

---

# Success Metrics

Examples:

* Daily Active Users
* Weekly Active Users
* Retention
* Conversion
* Revenue

Choose metrics that reflect value creation.

---

# Product Strategy Checklist

☐ Clear ICP

☐ Clear problem

☐ Clear positioning

☐ Core value identified

☐ Success metrics defined

☐ Distribution considered

A product without distribution is unfinished.
