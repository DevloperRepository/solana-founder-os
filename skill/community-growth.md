# Community Growth

## Purpose

Strong communities create durable advantages.

The goal is not to maximize member count.

The goal is to create a group of users who repeatedly create value for each other and the product.

---

# Community Flywheel

Users

# Community Stages

## Early Stage

Focus:

* Conversations
* Feedback
* User interviews
* Relationship building

Avoid:

* Over-moderation
* Complex structures

---

## Growth Stage

Focus:

* Ambassador programs
* Community events
* Educational initiatives
* User-generated content

---

## Mature Stage

Focus:

* Contributor programs
* Governance
* Leadership development

---

# Community Metrics

Track:

* Active members
* Returning members
* Contributor count
* Community-generated content
* Referral activity

Avoid vanity member counts.

---

# Ambassador Programs

Reward:

* Education
* Support
* Referrals
* Community leadership

Avoid rewarding spam activity.

# Field Notes

## Community Is Not A Telegram Group

A chat with 50,000 people is not necessarily a community.

A community exists when members create value for each other.

---

## Contributors Are More Valuable Than Spectators

A small group of highly engaged contributors often creates more value than a large passive audience.

---

## Moderation Is Not Community Building

Community building involves:

* Recognition
* Participation
* Shared goals

Moderation is only one small part.

---

## The Best Communities Create Identity

People stay because they feel connected to:

* A mission
* A culture
* A group

Identity drives retention.
