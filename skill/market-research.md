# Market Research

## Purpose

Market research helps founders understand where opportunities exist, who they are competing against, and whether a startup can realistically win.

The goal is not to find a market with no competitors.

The goal is to find a market where a meaningful advantage can be created.

---

# Research Objectives

Answer:

* How large is the opportunity?
* Who are the competitors?
* What are users already using?
* What gaps exist?
* Why now?

---

# Market Mapping

Categorize competitors into:

## Direct Competitors

Products solving the same problem.

Examples:

* Similar protocols
* Similar applications
* Similar marketplaces

## Indirect Competitors

Alternative ways users solve the problem.

Examples:

* Spreadsheets
* Telegram groups
* Existing workflows
* Traditional software

---

# Market Size Framework

## TAM

Total Addressable Market.

Maximum possible market size.

## SAM

Serviceable Available Market.

The portion realistically reachable.

## SOM

Serviceable Obtainable Market.

The share realistically captured.

---

# Competitive Analysis

For each competitor evaluate:

* Product
* Pricing
* Distribution
* Community
* Token model
* Strengths
* Weaknesses

Document findings in a comparison table.

---

# Market Timing

Questions:

* Is the market growing?
* Is infrastructure improving?
* Are user behaviors changing?
* Are regulations helping or hurting adoption?

Strong startups often emerge when timing changes.

---

# Positioning

Complete:

"For [target audience], our product is the [category] that [benefit], unlike [alternative], because [advantage]."
